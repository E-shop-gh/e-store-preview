Biography

Matthew Bartholomew Eshun was born on February 6, 1949, to the late Jacob Kofi Mbroh Eshun and Dora Aba Eduawa Appiah, both from Ekumfi Abor (Abodo). As the sixth born among 14 siblings, he was known for his boldness, care, profound wisdom, outspokenness and unwavering love for his family, friends and his faith.

He begun his basic education at Akumfi Abor M/A Primary and Middle School. He furthered his studies at Arthurs School of Draughtsmanship and Surveying in Agona Swedru. Upon completion, he migrated to Accra to live with his beloved late aunty Mercy Kudjo as he pursued for job.

He first worked at Public Works Department (PWD). Then moved to Cocoa Research Institute in Tafo, Bole in the Northern Region and Akim Afosu before setting up his professional business as a draughtsman and surveyor.

His passion for education and curiosity lead him to the Bible truth. On 7th June, 1974 he symbolized his dedication to Jehovah through water baptism in Accra.

Matthew B. Eshun was united in marriage with Grace Araba Kwarkyi. M.B. and Grace were blessed with seven children, four in-laws and twelve grandchildren.

He later became an entrepreneur to further Kingdom interest. He actively supported his congregation as a temporary pioneer now known as auxiliary pioneer where he became well-known and beloved.

Six months after his baptism, in 1975, he began serving as a regular pioneer and because of his enthusiasm and zeal for the kingdom work, he had the privilege to work with special pioneers, Joseph Akoto and Nathaniel Gbedemah, who were his close associates.

He was a familiar sight in the neighborhood – joyfully riding his motorcycle from place to place as he engaged in the preaching work that brought him so much fulfillment.

His zeal for the ministry was evident as a congregation elder, serving various congregations in Bole, Tafo, Afosu, Bubiashie, Teshie Twi, Airport View Twi and East Airport View Twi.

He was deeply loved by family, friends and publishers. He was known for his shepherding visits, where he offered heartfelt encouragement and spiritual guidance. He was selfless, humble, hardworking and very empathetic towards people. He always gave a listening ear to everyone who needed his attention whether in the family or the congregation.

He helped many brothers and sisters deepen their love for the field ministry, often motivating them to pursue the full-time ministry. Thus, many had the pioneering privilege including five (5) of his children due to his influence. Even in his old age, he remained an active regular pioneer, enthusiastically sharing the good news electronically. He fulfilled a faithful service for 51 years.

After battling illness for four (4) years, M.B. Eshun fell asleep in death on July, 24, 2025. Though his absence leaves a great void, our hearts find comfort in knowing that he now rests in Jehovah's perfect memory, awaiting the promised resurrection.

He was a shepherd, a teacher, a pioneer, a faithful husband, a devoted father and grandfather, a good friend and above all, a loyal servant of Jehovah. He will be dearly missed and fondly remembered.
